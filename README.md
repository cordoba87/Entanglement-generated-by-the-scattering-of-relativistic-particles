# Entanglement-generated-by-the-scattering-of-relativistic-particles
Bachelor's degree in physics final project.
In this Bachelor’s Thesis, the generation of entanglement in 2 ! 2 relativistic fermion
scattering processes is analyzed using the Wess-Zumino model in d = 4. With the kinematics
fixed in the center-of-mass reference frame, the final scattering state is modeled as a
pure bipartite two-qubit system, whose degrees of freedom are the helicities of the outgoing
particles. The degree of entanglement is quantified through the analytical and computational
calculation of the concurrence defined by Wootters. The two-component Weyl spinor
formalism is employed, and the tree-level scattering amplitudes are evaluated analytically
and computationally using Mathematica. The study analyzes three distinct regimes: the
asymptotic limit of massless fermions and scalars, the Soft Supersymmetry-Breaking introduced
by the addition of scalar mass, and the general massive regime (scalar and fermionic),
evaluating both the exact and broken supersymmetry cases. The results demonstrate that
for an initial state of opposite helicities j+􀀀i, the entanglement is maximal for any polar
angle in the massless case, and at  = =2 independently of the energy scale in the massive
cases. On the other hand, the concurrence for an incoming helicity state j++i is verified to
vanish in the high-energy limit, and to be non-vanishing in the massive regime with exact
supersymmetry.
